SECTION 01

PRIVACY POLICY

Last Updated: May 1, 2025

1.1 Introduction

Agarta ("we", "our", "the Platform") is an immersive educational and training platform operated by Hope Land Canada Inc. and developed with the technical support of Kimuntu Power Inc. We are committed to protecting the privacy, security, and integrity of your personal data.

This Privacy Policy applies to all users of the Agarta platform, including the website at agarta.ai, mobile applications, and VR/XR experiences. By accessing or using Agarta, you agree to this Privacy Policy.

Applicable Legal Frameworks: PIPEDA (Canada), applicable provincial privacy laws (Ontario, Quebec Law 25), COPPA (U.S. users), FERPA (U.S. educational institutions), CCPA (California users), and ISO/IEC 27001 & 27701 aligned security practices.

1.2 Information We Collect

1.2.1 Personal Information:

  • Full name and display name
  • Email address
  • Account credentials (hashed passwords only — never stored in plain text)
  • Educational or institutional affiliation
  • User role (student, teacher, administrator, trainer, partner)
  • Communication preferences and language settings

1.2.2 Technical and Usage Data:

  • IP address (anonymized after 30 days)
  • Device type, operating system, browser type and version
  • Log files, analytics events, crash reports
  • Session activity, navigation behavior, feature usage
  • VR interaction metrics (headset type, session duration, interaction events — if applicable)

1.2.3 Educational and Performance Data:

  • Assessment results and quiz scores
  • Learning progress and milestone tracking
  • Interaction logs within learning modules
  • Engagement time and content completion rates

1.2.4 Data We Do NOT Collect:

  • Financial information or payment card details (processed exclusively by third-party payment providers)
  • Biometric identifiers (facial recognition, fingerprints, voice prints)
  • Health or medical records
  • Precise geolocation without explicit user consent
  • Sensitive personal data unless legally required and institutionally authorized

1.3 How We Use Your Information

  • Provide, operate, and personalize the Agarta platform experience
  • Manage user authentication and access permissions
  • Deliver learning analytics, progress reports, and educational insights
  • Improve content accuracy, platform performance, and AI recommendations
  • Ensure platform security, fraud detection, and abuse prevention
  • Communicate service updates, new features, and technical support
  • Comply with applicable legal obligations and audit requirements
  • Generate anonymized, aggregated analytics for platform improvement

1.4 Legal Basis for Processing

ConsentExplicit user consent for non-essential data collection, marketing communications, and optional features
ContractProcessing necessary to provide the services described in the Terms & Conditions
Legitimate InterestSecurity monitoring, performance optimization, fraud prevention, and platform improvement
Legal ComplianceAudit trails, safety requirements, and regulatory reporting obligations

1.5 Data Sharing and Disclosure

Agarta does not sell personal data. We share data only in limited circumstances outlined below.

Approved Service Providers:

  • Secure cloud hosting providers (with data processing agreements)
  • Analytics providers (anonymized/aggregated data only)
  • Technical integration partners (limited to data necessary for the integration)

Educational Institutions:

If you access Agarta through a school, university, or organization, authorized administrators within that institution may access learner data relevant to their educational programs, subject to institutional agreements.

Legal Compliance:

We may disclose information when required by law, court order, government request, or to protect the safety of users or prevent unlawful activities.

1.7 Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to provide services, meet legal obligations, or maintain user accounts. Inactive accounts may be anonymized after 24 months of inactivity. Users may request deletion at any time (see Section 1.8).

1.8 Your Rights

  • Right of Access — Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right of Rectification — Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right of Erasure — Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Withdraw Consent — Withdraw previously granted consent
  • Right to Data Portability — Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Restrict Processing — Limit how we use your data
  • Right to Object — Object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at: contact@agarta.ai

1.9 International Data Transfers

Data may be processed and stored in Canada and, where required by infrastructure, in the United States. We implement appropriate safeguards including Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and data processing agreements compliant with applicable privacy frameworks.

SECTION 03

COOKIE POLICY

Last Updated: May 1, 2025

3.1 What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit Agarta. They help us provide a secure, functional, and personalized experience. We also use similar technologies such as local storage, session storage, and web beacons.

3.2 Types of Cookies We Use

EssentialRequired for login, security, session management, and navigation. Cannot be disabled without affecting core functionality.
FunctionalRemember user preferences, language settings, and accessibility options. Improve personalization without tracking behavior.
AnalyticsUnderstand platform performance and user behavior to improve features. Anonymized and do not identify individuals.
AdvertisingAgarta does NOT use marketing cookies, advertising trackers, or third-party ad networks. Our platform is advertising-free.

3.3 Cookie Consent & Control

  • First-visit banner allows you to accept all, reject non-essential, or customize preferences
  • Preferences can be modified at any time through the Agarta cookie settings panel
  • Browser-level cookie controls are also effective

Note: Disabling essential cookies will limit or prevent access to core platform features.

SECTION 05

CHILDREN'S PRIVACY NOTICE

This notice explains how Agarta handles data for users under the age of 13 (or under the minimum age defined by applicable local law).

5.1 Parental and Institutional Consent

Agarta requires verifiable parental consent (or consent through an educational institution) before collecting any personal data from children. Schools and institutions accessing Agarta on behalf of minor students serve as the authorized consent agents.

5.2 Child-Safe Design Principles

  • Age-appropriate content across all platform sections
  • No advertising of any kind directed at minors
  • Minimized data collection — only what is necessary for the educational service
  • No behavioral tracking for profiling of minors
  • Parental access rights to review, correct, or delete their child's data
  • Moderated VR and community experiences

5.3 Data Use for Minors

Data collected for minor users is used exclusively for educational purposes within the Agarta platform. It is never shared with third parties for commercial purposes, and never used for profiling or advertising.
